About

I am Amit Kumar Singh, an academician and researcher in the field of Computer Science and Engineering, with a strong interest in modern computer networks, programmable data planes, and intelligent network monitoring systems. My work primarily focuses on networking problems, especially in areas such as flow identification, in-band network telemetry, and performance analysis of next-generation networks.

I actively explore emerging technologies including Software Defined Networking (SDN), Network Function Virtualization (NFV), and 5G systems, and I enjoy building practical lab environments using virtualization platforms and Linux-based tools to support both research and teaching activities.

As a teacher, I believe in learning by implementation. I regularly develop study material, lab demonstrations, and hands-on exercises to help students understand complex networking and system concepts in a simple and practical manner. My goal is to bridge the gap between theoretical concepts and real-world deployment.

Research Interests

  • Computer Networks
  • In-Band Network Telemetry (INT)
  • Programmable Networks & P4
  • SDN and NFV Architectures
  • Network Performance Measurement & Traffic Analysis
  • 5G and Future Internet Architectures
  • Virtualization and Cloud-based Network Labs

I am always interested in collaboration, student mentoring, and research discussions related to intelligent networking systems and next-generation communication technologies.
